How to use Cheque Printer Pro

First-time setup per bank

  1. Pick the bank and currency.
  2. Enter test values. Turn on Show Bank Template to see the cheque outline.
  3. Turn on Edit Mode and drag each field to match a scan of your real cheque.
  4. Click Save Positions when done. This bank now remembers its layout.

Printing

  1. Load a blank cheque into your printer.
  2. Click Print. In the print dialog, set paper to custom 178×84mm (or A4 → manual position), margins None, headers/footers off.
  3. For plain-paper test prints, turn on Show Bank Template first.

Office workflow

  1. Click Save & New after each print — auto-increments cheque number.
  2. Open the Register tab to search, filter by bank/currency, and export to CSV for your accounts.
  3. All data is stored locally in your browser. Export regularly as backup.

Payee register

Save frequent payees once. While printing, type a name and pick from the suggestions — bank and currency auto-fill from the saved payee's defaults. Click ⊕ next to the payee field to save a new one. Manage all payees in the Payees tab — add, edit, delete, import/export CSV. Use the CSV import to bulk-load payees from your existing spreadsheet (required column: Name; optional: Account Number, Default Bank, Default Currency, Category, Notes).

Bilingual amounts

For MVR, the Dhivehi (Thaana) amount is generated automatically using the standard Sofwath/NumberToThaana vocabulary. Toggle Print Dhivehi line on cheque to include it in the printed output.

Undated cheques

Toggle Leave undated to skip the date. Choose to leave the date line completely blank, print a placeholder like __ / __ / ____, or write your own custom text.

Crossing / endorsement

Pick from presets (A/C Payee Only, Not Negotiable, & Co., etc.) or write your own. Choose a stamp style (boxed, rotated, parallel lines, plain) and position (corner, across top, across payee line, bottom). Your choice is saved per bank.
